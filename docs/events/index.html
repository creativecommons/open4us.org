Events

Please see the below list of webinars and events that OPEN Parters offer free of charge, tailored to support TAACCCT grantees and the development of their Open Educational Resources.

Past webinars are archived below, with links to recordings and handouts as applicable. Representatives from the OPEN Partners also create customized webinars for grantee institutions and consortia based on the various service areas. For more information and to inquire about a custom webinar for your TAACCCT project team, see the contact page.

Have a suggestion for a webinar?

Creative Commons is organizing a series of webinars focused on bringing together TAACCCT grantees for requesting and sharing curricula in common areas of interest. Separate webinars are being organized for each of:

  • Manufacturing
  • Health
  • Information Technology
  • Energy, and
  • Transportation

To get in touch about webinar services, please contact taa@creativecommons.org.

  • Length: 60 minutes
  • Featured Speakers: Boyoung Chae, Program Administrator at Washington State Board for Community and Technical Colleges
  • Description:The purpose of this session is to share the best practices for managing, organizing and delivering open development projects. An Overview of Creative Commons and Open Educational Resources for Faculty (February 2014)

  • Date: February 7, 2014 (1:00pmEDT / 10:00amPDT)
  • Length: 60 minutes

Open File Formats, Interoperability, and Tagging with Meta-data

      • Date: November 19, 2012
      • Length: 60 minutes
      • Featured Speaker: Greg Grossmeier, Creative Commons
      • Description: The TAACCCT program requires all grantees to develop learning materials such as books, courses, modules, seminars, tutoring systems, and simulations as Open Educational Resources (OER). All digital assets are expected to maximize interoperability, exchange and reuse. Grantees are expected to use industry-leading e-learning open standards and specifications. Greg Grossmeier leads us through open technical formats, interoperability standards and tagging with meta data.
